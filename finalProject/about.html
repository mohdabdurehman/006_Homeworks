Who I Am

From the Sofa
to the Platform

I wasn't always into fitness. In fact, I was the classic skinny-fat guy — looked okay in clothes but had no muscle definition, a soft midsection, and zero strength. I could barely do five push-ups. My arms looked like noodles, and I felt weak every single day.

The worst part? I wasn't skinny enough to be called "lean," and I wasn't big enough to be called "strong." I was stuck in this awkward middle ground where I felt invisible. Low confidence, low energy, and absolutely no direction when it came to training or nutrition.

Then one day, I looked in the mirror and decided I'd had enough. I didn't want to feel weak anymore. I didn't want to be the guy who avoids taking his shirt off at the beach. So I walked into the gym for the first time — nervous, clueless, and carrying more self-doubt than ambition.

"The day you step into the gym is the day you become someone who refuses to settle for less than their potential."

— My personal mantra

The first few months were brutal. I had no idea what I was doing. I bounced between workouts, ate randomly, and saw minimal progress. But instead of quitting, I started learning. I researched nutrition, understood progressive overload, and committed to showing up 5 days a week — no excuses.

Slowly but surely, the skinny-fat body started disappearing. My shoulders widened. My chest filled out. My arms finally started looking like they actually did something. I gained 18kg of quality muscle while staying lean. The transformation wasn't just physical — my mental health, discipline, and confidence skyrocketed.

Today, I train 5 days a week using a push/pull/legs split. I track my calories and protein, sleep 8 hours religiously, and treat fitness not as a chore — but as the anchor of my entire life. I'm no longer the weak, insecure guy on the couch. I'm the guy who proved that skinny-fat can become strong, muscular, and unstoppable.

This website is everything I wish I knew when I started. No fluff. No fake transformations. Just real, honest advice from someone who walked the same path you're on right now. If I can change, so can you.

Favourites

My Favourite Exercises

Deadlift

The king of all lifts. Nothing builds total-body strength, posture, and raw power like pulling heavy iron off the floor.

Squat

Full-depth squats are the foundation of lower body development. Master the squat and everything else follows.

Pull-Ups

The ultimate test of relative strength. Wide grip, close grip, weighted — pull-ups build the back of champions.

Bench Press

Still the best chest builder ever invented. The bench press is where strength is forged and egos are humbled.

Philosophy

What I Believe About Fitness

01
Consistency beats intensity. Showing up 5 days a week, even when you don't feel like it, beats sporadic "beast mode" sessions every single time.
02
Progress is not linear. You will have plateau weeks and off days. That doesn't mean you're failing — it means you're human. Keep going.
03
Nutrition is 70% of the battle. You can't out-train a bad diet. Eat enough protein, track your calories, and watch your body transform.
04
Sleep is a weapon. Eight hours of quality sleep is the most powerful performance-enhancing tool available to any athlete — and it's free.
05
Ego is the enemy. Leave it at the door. Check your form, use appropriate weight, and respect the process. Injuries end journeys.
06
Start where you are. You don't need a perfect plan or a fancy gym. You just need to start. The rest figures itself out.
