Nutrition &
Diet Guide

You can't out-train a bad diet. Everything you eat is either building your body or breaking it down. Choose wisely.

The Foundation

Understanding Macronutrients

Every food you eat is made of three macros. Master these and you master your body composition.

35%
Protein

The building block of muscle tissue. Aim for 1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight daily. Sources: chicken, eggs, fish, Greek yoghurt, whey, legumes.

45%
Carbohydrates

Your body's primary fuel source. Prioritise complex carbs for sustained energy. Sources: oats, rice, sweet potato, fruit, whole grains.

20%
Healthy Fats

Essential for hormone production, joint health, and vitamin absorption. Sources: avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds, fatty fish.

Calorie Math

Know Your Numbers

Calculate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) and eat in a slight surplus (+200–300 kcal) to build muscle, or a slight deficit (−300–500 kcal) to lose fat — while keeping protein high to preserve muscle.

My Daily Meal Plan

~2,800 kcal · 185g protein · 320g carbs · 80g fats — calibrated for muscle building at ~80kg bodyweight.

Smart Eating

10 Nutrition Tips That Changed Everything

01
Meal prep on Sundays. Spending 2 hours cooking on Sunday saves you from making bad food choices all week long. Cook in bulk, store in containers.
02
Protein at every meal. Aim to hit 30–40g of protein per meal to maximise muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.
03
Don't fear carbs. Carbohydrates are your training fuel. Timing them around your workout — before and after — optimises performance and recovery.
04
Drink water first. Often hunger is actually dehydration. Drink 500ml of water when you feel hungry and wait 10 minutes before eating.
05
Track for 30 days. Use an app like MyFitnessPal for one month to understand your eating habits. You'll be shocked by what you discover.
06
Eat whole foods first. 80% of your diet should come from minimally processed, single-ingredient foods. Save the 20% for life and sanity.
07
Don't skip breakfast. A high-protein breakfast sets the tone for the whole day and reduces cravings and overeating later on.
08
Slow down when eating. It takes 20 minutes for satiety signals to reach your brain. Eating slowly means you eat less without feeling deprived.
