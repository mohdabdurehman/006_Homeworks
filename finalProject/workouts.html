Before You Begin

How to Use These Programs

Always Warm Up

Spend 5–10 minutes on light cardio and dynamic stretches before every session. Cold muscles injure easily.

Respect Rest Times

Rest periods are part of the programme. Too short and you can't perform; too long and you lose intensity.

Track Your Lifts

Write down every set and weight. Progressive overload — doing more than last time — is how muscles grow.

Recovery

Train Hard. Recover Harder.

Sleep 8 Hours

Muscle is built while you sleep, not while you lift. 7–9 hours of quality sleep is non-negotiable for recovery and growth hormone release.

Hydrate Daily

Aim for 3–4 litres of water on training days. Dehydration reduces strength, focus, and recovery speed significantly.

Rest Days Matter

Active recovery — walks, stretching, foam rolling — on off days keeps blood flowing and muscles repairing without added stress.
