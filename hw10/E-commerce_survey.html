Supporting Market Research (2025-2026)

Mobile Dominance

According to Pew Research (via ReadyCloud, 2026), 91% of U.S. adults own a smartphone and 98% own a cellphone [citation:1]. eMarketer reports that mobile devices accounted for 56.4% of online transactions during the 2025 holiday season [citation:7].

Market Size & Growth

eMarketer data shows U.S. retail ecommerce sales reached $1.2337 trillion in 2025, with ecommerce representing 16.4% of total retail sales [citation:1]. Global ecommerce is projected to hit $6.419 trillion in 2025 [citation:4].

Marketplace Growth

Research from Mirakl (2026) indicates that marketplaces like Amazon are growing 4.5x faster than traditional ecommerce, driven by built-in discovery and high-intent traffic [citation:8].

Conclusions & Analysis

Based on the survey results and supporting data from Pew Research and eMarketer, the following conclusions can be drawn:

  1. E-commerce is Universal: The finding that 100% of participants have purchased online aligns with national trends showing ecommerce as a deeply embedded behavior, with 98% of adults owning cellphones and many online "almost constantly" [citation:1].
  2. Frequency Drives Revenue: The majority (50%) shop monthly. This "subscription-like" habit supports Adobe data showing consistent year-over-year growth (5.3% in Q4 2025) [citation:1].
  3. The "Amazon Effect": 70% preferring Amazon validates market research showing marketplaces growing 4.5x faster than traditional ecommerce due to convenience and Prime ecosystems [citation:8].
  4. Mobile is the Present: With 91% smartphone penetration and over half of holiday sales on mobile, the survey's demographic (50% age 26-39) represents the core mobile-first consumer base driving the industry [citation:7].
  5. Education & Spending: The high rate of college graduates (60%) in the survey suggests a correlation between higher education, disposable income, and comfort with digital transactions, a key target demographic for D2C brands.

📌 Final Takeaway: Ecommerce is no longer an "alternative" shopping method but the primary retail channel for most consumers under 40, driven by mobile accessibility and marketplace convenience.
